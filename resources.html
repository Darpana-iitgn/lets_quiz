Book Recommendations

When it comes to quizzes, good old books are your best friend and a great source of knowledge compared to websites. Here are some great book recommendations to get you started. What If: Serious Scientific Answers to Absurd Hypothetical Questions is a 2014 non-fiction book by Randall Munroe in which the author answers hypothetical science questions sent to him by readers of his webcomic, xkcd.

The Ultimate Bournvita Quiz Contest Book: The Ultimate Bournvita Quiz Contest Book of Knowledge - Vol. 1 is one of the two volumes of books that compiles almost all of the questions that are asked on the quiz show "The Bournvita Quiz Contest".

Britannica: Encyclopædia Britannica, the oldest continously updated English-language general encyclopaedia, maintained by Encyclopædia Britannica, Incorporation.

The Big Trivia Quiz Book: An addictive quiz book for all the family featuring 10,000 questions and featuring 10 different general knowledge categories, The Big Quiz Book has something for everyone.

The Book of General Ignorance: The Book of General Ignorance is the first in a series of books based on the final round in the intellectual British panel game QI, written by series-creator John Lloyd and head-researcher John Mitchinson, to help spread the QI philosophy of curiosity to the reading public.

Quizzing in pop culture

Quizzing has had a significant presence in Indian pop culture, often portrayed as both a test of knowledge and a source of entertainment. From iconic TV quiz shows that have become household names to personalities who have made quizzing more popular, the culture of quizzing has inspired many to challenge their intellect while being entertained. Quizzing has been featured in movies, TV shows, and even celebrity-hosted programs, cementing its place in Indian entertainment.

Amitabh Bachchan: Host of Kaun Banega Crorepati (Indian version of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?)

Derek O’Brien: Host of the long-running quiz show Bournvita Quiz Contest

Quiz Time: A popular quiz show in the 1980s and 90s hosted by Siddhartha Basu
